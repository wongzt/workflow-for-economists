# 5W1H Framework for Empirical Economics

Use `5W1H` to pressure-test whether a research idea is specific enough to become an empirical project.

## What

- What policy, institution, shock, or market mechanism is being studied?
- What exact treatment or exposure varies?
- What is the outcome that matters economically?

## Why

- Why does the question matter for welfare, policy design, or market behavior?
- Why is the setting informative rather than merely convenient?
- Why would the answer change what economists think they know?

## Who

- Who is treated, exposed, or affected?
- Who forms the comparison group?
- Who might respond differently across margins?

## When

- When does treatment begin?
- When should effects appear if the mechanism is real?
- When do anticipation, lagged adjustment, or policy overlap complicate timing?

## Where

- Where does the variation come from geographically or institutionally?
- Where might external validity break down?

## How

- How is the identifying variation generated?
- How will the treatment and outcome be measured?
- How will the paper distinguish the main mechanism from competing channels?
