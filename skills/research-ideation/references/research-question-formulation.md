# Research Question Formulation

Good empirical economics questions are narrow, testable, and tied to a source of variation.

## Weak Form

- "How does digital access matter?"

## Stronger Form

- "Does county-level broadband rollout raise rural employment by improving job search and outside-option access?"

## Checklist

- treatment is explicit
- outcome is explicit
- comparison group is implicit or stated
- timing is clear
- contribution is not just "new setting"
- identifying variation can be named in one sentence

## Final Test

If the question cannot be paired with a concrete design and dataset, it is still a topic, not yet a project.
