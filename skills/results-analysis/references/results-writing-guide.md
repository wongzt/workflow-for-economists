# Results Writing Guide

The results section should answer three questions quickly:

1. what is estimated
2. what the coefficient means economically
3. how the result behaves across the main specification sequence

## Good Practice

- introduce each table by its role in the argument
- interpret sign and magnitude, not just significance
- explain why coefficients move across columns
- keep mechanism claims narrower than the main treatment-effect claim
- route unsettled interpretation to `theory-auditor`

## Avoid

- rewriting the methods section
- hiding sample changes
- turning heterogeneity into proof of channel
- using inflated prose to compensate for weak design logic
