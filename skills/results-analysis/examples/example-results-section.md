# Example Results Section

## Main Results

Table 2 reports the baseline estimates of tuition-free vocational-school expansion on youth employment. Column 1 includes county and year fixed effects. Column 2 adds time-varying demographic controls. Column 3 restricts the sample to counties with stable reporting coverage. The coefficient remains positive across all three columns and moves only modestly as controls and sample restrictions are added.

In the preferred specification, the policy raises the youth employment rate by 1.8 percentage points relative to a pre-policy mean of 41 percent. The magnitude is economically meaningful, but the interpretation depends on the validity of the rollout timing assumption.

Figure 3 presents the event-study estimates. Pre-treatment coefficients are close to zero and imprecisely estimated, which is consistent with the identifying assumption. The post-treatment pattern rises gradually rather than immediately, matching a channel in which school expansion affects labor-market outcomes with a lag.

## Heterogeneity

Table 3 shows that the effect is larger in counties with low pre-policy vocational-school capacity. This pattern is consistent with a capacity-relief mechanism, though it does not by itself rule out differential local policy effort.

## Interpretation Boundary

The current evidence supports the claim that the policy improved youth employment in exposed counties. The mechanism evidence is suggestive rather than decisive, so any stronger claim should be routed through a theory audit and additional mechanism tests.
