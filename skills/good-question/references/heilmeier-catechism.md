# Heilmeier Catechism

Use this card for grant ideas, proposal abstracts, project pitches, lab plans, and reviewer-style stress tests.

Source: DARPA, "The Heilmeier Catechism". https://www.darpa.mil/about/heilmeier-catechism

## Core Idea

Force a research idea to explain what it will do, why current approaches are inadequate, why the new approach might work, who cares, what risks exist, and how progress will be measured.

## Adapted Questions

1. What are you trying to learn or make possible?
2. How is this done or understood today, and what are the limits?
3. What is the new idea, assumption, mechanism, dataset, or intervention?
4. Why might it succeed now?
5. Who cares: theorists, practitioners, policymakers, users, ecosystems, patients, or methods communities?
6. What are the main risks and how will you retire them?
7. What evidence would count as success, partial success, or failure?
8. How long will it take, what will it cost, and what are the intermediate milestones?

## Stress-Test Output

```markdown
**Claim:** ...
**Current limit:** ...
**New approach:** ...
**Why now:** ...
**Stakeholders:** ...
**Risks:** ...
**Milestones:** ...
**Kill criterion:** ...
```

## Red Flags

- The proposal describes activities, not a question.
- Success metrics are vague or only internal to the method.
- Risks are listed but not connected to mitigation or decision gates.
- The project would still be pitched the same way if the main hypothesis were false.
